1.	How do I approach designing software?
o	What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?
When approaching software design, I focus on breaking a problem down into manageable components and identifying both functional and visual requirements early in the process. For this project, I began by analyzing the reference image and determining which real‑world objects could be represented using simple geometric primitives. This helped guide my overall scene layout and ensured that my design choices aligned with the project constraints. One new design skill I strengthened during this project was the ability to think in terms of modular, reusable components rather than individual objects. By designing toys using repeated shapes and patterns, such as evenly spaced Ferris wheel carriages and cone‑based star spikes, I was able to create more complex forms while keeping the design efficient and organized.

o	What design process did you follow for your project work? How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?
The design process I followed was iterative and incremental. I began with a rough layout of the scene and focused on getting object placement and scale correct before refining details. Once the core shapes were established, I added textures, lighting, and transparency to improve realism. This step‑by‑step approach allowed me to isolate issues and make targeted improvements without overwhelming the development process. The same design tactics of starting simple, validating functionality early, and refining incrementally can be applied to future projects, especially those involving complex systems or visual components.


2.	How do I approach developing programs?
o	What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene? How did iteration factor into your development?
When approaching program development, I prioritize readability, modularity, and reusability. During this project, I made consistent use of custom functions to manage transformations, materials, textures, and lighting. New development strategies I applied included using loops and mathematical calculations to generate repeated geometry efficiently, such as the Ferris wheel carriages and the star‑shaped rattle head. This reduced redundancy and made the code easier to maintain. Iteration played a major role in development, as many features, such as camera control, object placement, transparency, and lighting, were refined multiple times based on visual results and testing.

o	How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project’s completion?
Early work focused primarily on making objects appear correctly, while later stages emphasized organization, user interaction, and visual polish. By the end of the project, I was thinking more intentionally about user experience, camera navigation, projection modes, and code scalability rather than simply rendering objects on the screen.
